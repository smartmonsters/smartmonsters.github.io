NPC Merchants

They can't be controlled by anyone, only obey network rules and are generally immortal. For added security, on mainnet before release, all NPCs would be transferred to a coin-eater address.

Merchants earn coins when players buy items. After a while, coins from that cash flow accumulates and the "organization of all players" can choose to spend them (or burn them) through majority vote.


Bounty and voting system

Any player can request that all players vote of whether a specified bounty is granted. Format (console command):

name_update Alice {\"msg_request\":\"1000\",\"msg_fee\":\"20\",\"msg_comment\":\""some text, perhaps link to forum post"\"}


In this example, Alice requests that a NPC sends 1000 coins to hunter Alice. If not specified, the fee is 1% of the requested amount. Note the extra quotes in msg_comment, they are needed if the text contains any spaces.

The fee is paid immediately to the NPC. Alice will get rations for it, so that the fee slowly refunds itself.

Every 5 rounds (10000 blocks) the network chooses the request with the highest fee, and it goes into voting, which also lasts 10000 blocks. To vote yay:

name_update Bob {\"msg_vote\":\"1000\"}


or nay

name_update Bob {\"msg_vote\":\"0\"}


Or anything in between. It's not possible to vote for less than 0 or more than the requested amount. If the median vote is 0 or the requested amount, then the actual bounty is the median (otherwise the average)

Voters are rewarded with 3 rations, plus 2 rations for each heart soldier. The "soft" population limit imposed by the price per ration would be doubled if all players vote regularly.


Informal voting

The bounty can be symbolic and less than the fee. In this case it's a simple Yes/No voting.


Formal voting

Network logic can parse the following voting proposals:


"Upkeep shall be higher!"
"Upkeep shall be lower!"
This indirectly determines the maximum number of players the game world can sustain.

Change will be in effect immediately after a non-zero bounty is granted to the address that proposed the voting. Upkeep can never be higher than 6 coins per 2000 blocks. Default setting is 6 / 2 = 3 coins. The first "lower" means 6 / 3 = 2 coins, then 1.5 coins, 1.2 coins and so on, with no lower limit.


"Increase the population limit!"
"Reduce the population limit!"
This determines the maximum number of players, including those who have parked their characters ("on vacation").

Change will be in effect immediately after a non-zero bounty is granted to the address that proposed the voting. Default setting is 50000, and voting can change it in increments of 10000. No upper or lower limit: 0 would mean there is no vacation for players and all have to pay upkeep all of the time.


"All nodes must upgrade!"
To authorize and enforce hardforks.

Change will be in effect immediately after a non-zero bounty is granted to the address that proposed the voting. Increments the minimum version, e.g. from v2.2.02 to v2.2.03, or from v2.2.03 to v2.2.04. Older nodes get a warning while the voting is in progress, then stop processing blocks.


msg_comment field is used to initiate the voting, for example:
name_update Alice {\"msg_request\":\"1\",\"msg_fee\":\"20\",\"msg_comment\":\""Upkeep shall be lower!"\"}


Burning coins

Coins currently owned by NPCs can be burnt according to player vote. This is achieved by granting a bounty to a general that has been sent on a path that leads to despawn and then transferred to a coin-eater address.

Another special case is feeding coins (currently owned by NPC merchants) back into the game: the bounty can go to a permanently monsterified character, perhaps one that is a member of the weakest color (the monster will level up from the extra coins).
