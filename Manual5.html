Glossary


SynonymsDefinition
Player Character Player,
"General"		 A unit that was created with a new unique name. They are assets on the SmartMonster blockchain, are controlled by (the private key of) an address, like coins, and can be sent to another address just like coins. When we say "Players can do x" the meaning is that "owners of a Player Character can do x, but not coin holders. Coin holders can however create new PCs.
Playernameaddress Name Address When a new Player Character with unique name is created, an amount of coins is locked under this address, and a data field of 1 constant string and 1 variable string ("Name", "Value") is created in the blockchain, containing the PC's name, and all moves of that player. Locked Coins are refunded if the PC dies on the spawnstrip.
Bank Spawnstrip Banking means stepping on one of the colored tiles in the 4 map corners.
This will send all looted coins to the wallet (i.e. player's reward address, or name address if no reward address exists) and the unit's character level is reset to 1. If a unit is staying on the spawnstrip for 30 blocks, it dies and all locked coins are sent to the wallet too.
Reward address and Name address can be set with "Config..." button.>
Heart Soldiers Additional units can be gained by running over a heart. These units don't have unique names and don't need coins to be created. They die if their General dies.
Heart They spawn semi-randomly near coins, and only in combat zones where movement is automatic
(so no one can farm them manually or with a bot)
Transfer Generals can be transferred to a new address, taking all their Heart Soldiers with them.
Useful for sending unit to a new wallet.
Color Color Team,
Color Faction		 All characters are either on yellow, red, green or blue.
There is generally no team damage (can't hurt members of same color, not even monsters)
In combat zones, all units will automatically attack other colors, or flee from them if outmatched.
Gamestate Cached data, extracted from the node's blockchain and always kept in memory.
Snapshots of the gamestate are saved in "game.dat", if this file is deleted, the node will regenerate it by replaying the blockchain.
In case of a blockchain re-org, an older gamestate snapshot is fetched and updated by reading all newer blocks.

Gamestates never communicate with gamestates of other nodes, all information they get comes from their own node's blockchain.
