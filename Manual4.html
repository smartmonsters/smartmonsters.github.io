Coin specs and POW mining for testnet

Block time: 1 minute
Address format: compatible with bitcoin, mainnet addresses start with "1"
Default port: 8396 (testnet 18396), RPC port: 8397 (testnet 18397), Core wallet RPC port: 8395 (testnet 18395)
Block reward: initially 10 coins/block, halving every 4 years



Testnet POW mining can be done from within the Qt-client: in Debug Window, use "setgenerate true" to start mining, and "setgenerate false" to stop. To switch from default algo sha256 to scrypt, put "algo=scrypt" in smartmonsters.conf. POW mining is needed to generate blocks, the game can't do this, even if it generates most of the coins.


Addendum (v2.2.05 - 2.2.07)


  • Players saved by their Amulet or Ring of Life Saving go into vacation mode instead of being teleported to their color's base. (Rationale: make it easier to just skip playing for some time without penalty)
  • Characters on vacation are ignored when determining player count and player combat score for balancing against monsters.
  • Characters on vacation forget their travel order.
  • Pathfinding for player characters will always check both ways of their color's 2-way teleporter and use the shortest (not necessarily least dangerous) path to and from battlefield areas.
  • Resurrected monsters start in their color's base instead of a semi-random harvest area.
  • Four "Starting Zones" for low level characters have been created near the map borders, equally distant to color bases. Everyone can enter, but higher level chars will be temporarily reduced to level 3.
  • Arbalests have always a strength of 2, and a range of 4 tiles if wielded by level 3 characters.
  • Staff of the Reaper limited to strength 2 if fired at max range.
  • Locked coin amount when creating a new player character now depends on block height, going down every 10000 blocks from 100 coins at block 0-10000 to 20 coins at block 50000.
  • Players can't initiate voting if their General is a monster.
  • Abolish death tax for dropped loot and for coins from banking.
  • "Dungeon Levels". Dlevels are vertically stacked copies of the original two-dimensional map, can be spawned through the voting system.
  • If more than 1 dlevel exits, they will freeze and unfreeze in turn so that combat only happens on one level at a time.
  • "dlevel=..." config option to display a specified dlevel. Players and coins on other levels appear semi-transparent.
  • Dynamic balancing and population control is now seperate for each dlevel.


Wishlist

In order to own assets outside of its blockchain, the DAO will need trusted workers, who are paid via the Bounty and voting system, and who can post collateral to the DAO.

Features adapted from 'BetterQT wallet for Huntercoin' (Testnet in a box here):
- A decentralized oracle that allows all players to participate and get rewarded.
- Creation of at least one fiat-pegged stablecoin backed by coins as collateral that can be traded from within the game.
- A combination of p2p and peer2contract trading, with zero or very low fees.
- The "contract" would take the form of an AI controlled market maker that is part of the coin's consensus.
- A link between the voting system (that is also part of the consensus) and the AI market maker with the goal of reducing volatility.

More power to the voting system:
- Control the overall randomness. Should the game play like a simulationist RPG or a deterministic super-chess?
- Balance of all players vs all monsters. More and stronger monster is a way to make coins scarcer, but there would be more loot...
- Other possibilities are the choice to allow/disallow merge mining, to pause/resume the entire game, and many more.
